About me
-----
I have a PhD in statistics from Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität (LMU) Munich in cooperation with Helmholtz Munich.
There, I focused on statistical methods for compositional analysis of high-throughput amplicon and single-cell data.
Sepcifically, I was interested in compositional data, differential abundance testing, tree-aggregated analysis, as well as Bayesian and non-Bayesian optimization.
I also worked on establishing and automating best-practice statistical workflows of scRNA-seq data from eucaryotes and procaryotes.

Currently, I am a CMC Statistician for Boehringer Ingelheim, bringing Bayesian ideas into bioprocess development.

When I'm not working on biostatistics, I like to spend my time doing all kinds of sports, volunteering as a swimming coach, or organizing the StuStaCulum festival in Munich.
